Cardiovascular Surgery

Cardiac surgery is a branch of medicine that plays a critical role in treating heart diseases and improving heart function. The heart is one of the most important organs in the body, and its proper functioning is essential for all organs and tissues to function healthily. Heart diseases are one of the most common causes of death worldwide, and cardiac surgery offers an important solution in the treatment of these diseases.

Brain and Nerve Surgery

Neurosurgery is a medical discipline that involves the diagnosis and treatment of diseases related to the brain, spinal cord and peripheral nervous system. The nervous system works like a network that provides communication with all organs and tissues of the body. Therefore, any problem that occurs in this system can greatly affect the quality of life of individuals. Neurosurgery plays a critical role in the treatment of various diseases that affect the nervous system and can be applied more effectively and safely thanks to developing medical technologies.

Dermatology

Dermatology is a medical discipline that deals with the diagnosis, treatment and prevention of diseases of the external structures of the body such as skin, hair, nails and mucosal membranes. The skin is the largest organ in the body and undertakes vital tasks such as protecting the body from external factors, preventing water loss and regulating body temperature. Therefore, the health of the skin is a reflection of the general state of health. Dermatology plays an important role in solving problems related to skin diseases and is critical to living a healthy life.

Orthopedics

Orthopedics is a medical discipline that specializes in the body's skeletal system and organs related to movement. This field covers all structures that enable the body to move, such as bones, joints, muscles, ligaments, tendons, and nerves. Orthopedics aims to treat disorders of these structures that result from congenital diseases, injuries, infections, aging, and other health problems. With both surgical and nonsurgical treatments, it is possible to ensure that orthopedic patients live a healthier and more functional life.
